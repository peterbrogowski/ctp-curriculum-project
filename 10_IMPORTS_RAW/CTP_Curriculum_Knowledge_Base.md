# 1. Organ Preservation Overview

Organ preservation is the process of maintaining the viability of donor
organs between procurement and transplantation.

Primary goals:

• Prevent cellular injury\
• Reduce metabolic demand\
• Maintain tissue integrity\
• Enable safe transport of organs

Two major preservation strategies:

  Method                Description
  --------------------- --------------------------------------------------
  Static Cold Storage   Organ stored on ice using preservation solution
  Machine Perfusion     Organ perfused with oxygenated solution or blood

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Cold Ischemic Time Reference

Cold ischemic time (CIT) is the duration between organ cooling and
reperfusion in the recipient.

  Organ       Typical Maximum CIT
  ----------- ---------------------
  Heart       4--6 hours
  Lung        6--8 hours
  Liver       8--12 hours
  Pancreas    12--18 hours
  Kidney      24--36 hours
  Intestine   6--8 hours

These values may vary depending on donor quality and preservation
methods.

# 3. Preservation Solutions

Common preservation solutions used during organ recovery.

  Solution                       Typical Use
  ------------------------------ --------------------------------
  University of Wisconsin (UW)   Liver, pancreas, kidney
  HTK (Custodiol)                Heart, liver
  Celsior                        Heart and lung
  Perfadex                       Lung
  Belzer MPS                     Machine perfusion preservation

Key characteristics of preservation solutions:

• Osmotic agents to prevent edema\
• Buffers to maintain pH\
• Electrolyte composition that stabilizes cells\
• Antioxidants and metabolic substrates

# 4. Machine Perfusion Types

Machine perfusion technologies are increasingly used to preserve organs.

  Method                                 Temperature
  -------------------------------------- -------------
  Normothermic Machine Perfusion (NMP)   35--37°C
  Hypothermic Machine Perfusion (HMP)    4--10°C
  Subnormothermic Perfusion              20--34°C

Examples of devices:

• OrganOx Metra -- normothermic liver perfusion\
• XVIVO systems -- lung perfusion (EVLP)\
• TransMedics OCS -- heart, lung, liver transport perfusion\
• LifePort -- kidney perfusion

# 5. Organ Recovery Basics

Key steps during organ recovery:

1.  Donor identification verification
2.  Surgical exposure
3.  Cannulation
4.  Cross-clamp
5.  Cold flush with preservation solution
6.  Organ removal
7.  Back table preparation

Recovery teams must maintain sterile technique and ensure accurate
documentation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Aseptic Technique

Sterility is critical during organ recovery.

Key principles:

• Surgical hand antisepsis • Sterile gowning and gloving • Proper use of
personal protective equipment (PPE) • Maintaining sterile fields •
Sterile transfer of solutions and instruments

Breaks in sterile technique can compromise organ viability.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Specimen Collection

Specimens collected during organ recovery may include:

  Specimen        Purpose
  --------------- -------------------------------------
  Blood samples   Infectious disease testing
  Lymph nodes     Tissue typing
  Spleen          HLA typing
  Vessels         Reconstruction or graft preparation

Specimens must be labeled according to OPTN policy and transported under
appropriate conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Organ Packaging Standards

Organs are packaged using a **triple sterile bag technique**.

Typical packaging process:

1.  Organ placed in sterile inner bag with preservation solution
2.  Inner bag sealed and placed into second sterile bag
3.  Second bag sealed and placed in third bag
4.  Triple bag placed in sterile container with ice

Transport containers must:

• Maintain temperature • Prevent contamination • Include required
documentation

# 10. Transplant Terminology

Common terms used in transplant preservation.

  Term            Definition
  --------------- -------------------------------------------------------------
  Cold Ischemia   Lack of blood flow while organ is cooled
  Warm Ischemia   Lack of perfusion at body temperature
  Cross-clamp     Surgical clamping of aorta to stop circulation
  Flush           Infusion of preservation solution through organ vasculature
  Back Table      Surgical preparation of organ before implantation
